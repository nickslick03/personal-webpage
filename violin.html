Violin

You only need to practice on the days that you eat.

- Suzuki

The violin was Nicholas' 1st instrument and may be his only instrument (besides singing). He doesn't mind, since the violin is really stinkin' hard and he will always need to practice. He started playing as soon as he could walk, with his mother as his teacher. Growing up listening to lots of classical music, Nicholas has come to enjoy the many great works he was taught.

Nicholas Joined YOCJ in the fall of 2016, where he played a variety of orchestral works. He played in all the orchestras the program had to offer from String Prepatory, to Pro Arte, to Symphony Orchesta. His favorite piece by far was Dvorak's 9th Symphony, which he played his senior year. He sat 5th chair in the 1st violin section of the Symphony Orchestra his senior year.

Nicholas Currently takes lessons with Peter Sirotin which has a challenging experience (for both parties). He loves it through and finds his newfound skills make playing in Messiah's Symphony Orchestra an awesome time. Nicholas currently sits 4th chair in the 1st Violin section.
